# Public Contact And Support Model

## Current State

All public contact channels are placeholders. They are not monitored and must not be rendered as `mailto:` or `tel:` links. No form sends data and no response-time commitment is made.

## Escalation Model Placeholders

General support, account safety, privacy requests, accessibility barriers, legal notices, and active security incidents require separate approved owners and escalation paths. Public copy must not route an incident through an unmonitored general channel or promise a response window that Operations has not approved.

## Incident Versus General Support

The public contact center is informational only. It does not implement incident intake, emergency response, authentication recovery, legal notice delivery, or private transaction support. Those paths require approved systems, staffing, data handling, and operating procedures before publication.

## Publication Requirements

Privacy and accessibility contacts require legal/privacy/accessibility review. Every published channel must be organization-controlled, public-safe, monitored, documented, and approved for its stated purpose. Personal email addresses, private phone numbers, residential addresses, credentials, and identifiers are prohibited.

## Publication Checklist

- [ ] Category owner and backup owner approved
- [ ] Organization-controlled channel approved and monitored
- [ ] Privacy classification and retention reviewed
- [ ] Accessibility and language reviewed
- [ ] Escalation and incident separation documented
- [ ] No unapproved service-level or response-time promise
- [ ] Legal publication gate passes
- [ ] Final deployment authorization granted separately

No live support, email delivery, ticketing, chat, analytics, or consent integration is included.
